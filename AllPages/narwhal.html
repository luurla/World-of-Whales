    Life on the edge of the ice

    Narwhals are medium-sized toothed whales that live in the Arctic waters of Canada, Greenland, Norway, and Russia. They are one of two species of whale in the Monodontidae family, along with the beluga whale.

    THREATS TO THE LIFE OF THE NARWhal

    Narwhals are considered a near-threatened species due to the impacts of climate change and hunting by indigenous people for subsistence. In addition, they are vulnerable to disturbance from noise pollution, oil and gas exploration, and shipping traffic in their Arctic habitat.

    Narwhals are known for their unique, spiraled tusk , which is actually an elongated upper left canine tooth that can grow up to 10 feet long. The purpose of the tusk is not fully understood, but it may be used for communication, sensory perception, or breaking through ice.
