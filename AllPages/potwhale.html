    Sperm whales are named for the waxy substance called spermaceti that was found in their head cavities. Spermaceti was highly prized by whalers for its use in candles, lubricants, and other products.

    Sperm whales are highly social animals that live in groups, or "pods" that can contain dozens of individuals. They communicate with each other using a variety of clicks and whistles, and have been known to coordinate their dives and hunting strategies.

    Sperm whales feed primarily on squid, but will also eat fish and other marine organisms. They have a unique hunting strategy, where they will dive deep and use echolocation to locate their prey, then rapidly ascend to the surface to catch them off guard. The loud clicking sounds they make during this process can be heard for miles underwater.
