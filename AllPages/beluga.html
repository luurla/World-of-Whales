    Beluga

    Beluga whales, also known as white whales, are toothed whales found in the Arctic and sub-Arctic regions of the world. They are known for their distinctive white coloring and rounded forehead, or melon, which is used for echolocation.

    Beluga whales are opportunistic feeders that will eat a variety of prey, including fish, squid, shrimp, and even crabs and clams. They are also known to suck up sediment from the seafloor and filter out small organisms like amphipods and copepods.

    Beluga whales are social animals that live in groups, or pods, that can contain up to several hundred individuals. They communicate with each other using a variety of vocalizations, including whistles, clicks, and chirps.
