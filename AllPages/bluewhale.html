    Blue Whales

    Blue whales are the largest animals on the planet, with adults reaching lengths of up to 30.5 meters and weights of up to 200 tons. Their heart alone can weigh as much as a car and is the largest known heart of any animal.

    Blue whales are found in all the world's oceans but are most commonly seen in colder waters such as the Antarctic and North Pacific. They migrate long distances between feeding and breeding grounds, traveling up to 16,000 kilometers each year.

    Blue whales are filter feeders that eat krill, a type of small crustacean. They can consume up to 4 tons of krill per day during peak feeding season, which lasts from late spring to early fall. Blue whales are known for their distinctive blue-gray coloring, mottled with lighter patches on the back and sides. They also have a broad, U-shaped head and a long, streamlined body that helps them to move efficiently through the water.
